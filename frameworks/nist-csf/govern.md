# NIST CSF 2.0 — Govern (GV)

**New in CSF 2.0.** Establishes the organizational risk management strategy, expectations, and governance structures that inform all other functions.

## Purpose

Govern is the umbrella that enables the other five functions. Without it, security activities are reactive and disconnected from business risk. It answers: *How does the organization understand and manage cybersecurity risk as part of its overall mission?*

## Key Outcomes (NIST CSF 2.0 Categories)

- **GV.OC** — Organizational Context: understand mission, stakeholders, and regulatory obligations
- **GV.RM** — Risk Management Strategy: risk tolerance defined and communicated
- **GV.RR** — Roles, Responsibilities & Authorities: cybersecurity accountability assigned
- **GV.PO** — Policy: cybersecurity policy established and enforced
- **GV.OV** — Oversight: results reviewed and used to improve risk management
- **GV.SC** — Cybersecurity Supply Chain Risk Management: suppliers assessed and monitored

## Vendor Architecture Coverage

**Strong:** CISA ZTMM (Governance cross-cutting capability)  
**Partial:** Microsoft MCRA (Purview Compliance, information governance)  
**Weak/None:** Palo Alto ZTA, Zscaler ZTE, CrowdStrike Falcon, Google CSA, AWS SRA, Cisco

> Note: Govern is consistently the least covered function by vendor security architectures. Dedicated GRC platforms (Archer, ServiceNow GRC, Vanta) fill this gap — they are not marketed as cybersecurity vendors but are essential to operationalizing Govern.
